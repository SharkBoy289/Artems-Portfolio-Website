## A learning lab, not a single app

Over 2024–2025 I kept a running monorepo of experiments — the place I go to
try a new framework or chase an idea. It's intentionally broad rather than
deep, and it's where a lot of my self-teaching happens:

- **Graphics & WebGL:** a Three.js Minecraft-style voxel experiment, a solar
  system renderer, and D3.js data-type visualizers.
- **Web apps:** an Angular 18 app with SSR, a Next.js pathways explorer, and a
  full-stack web scraper (Express + Puppeteer + TypeORM).
- **AI & algorithms:** a pet-classification model experiment, a chess
  problem-solver, and data-analysis utilities in Python.
- **Native & tooling:** Swift habit-tracker and word-widget prototypes, a
  to-do app, and a scientific calculator.

## Why it's here

No single one of these is a flagship — and I'm not going to pretend otherwise.
Together they show something more useful: consistent, self-driven learning
across very different stacks. The standouts get promoted to full projects as I
take them further.
