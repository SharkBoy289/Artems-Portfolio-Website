## What it does

Job Dashboard turns the chaos of a job search into a single, always-visible
pipeline. Recruiter and ATS email (Greenhouse, Lever, Ashby, Workday, …) is
labelled in Gmail, picked up by a scheduled pipeline, and **classified by
Claude with structured output** into a clean set of stages:

`planned → applied → screen → technical_interview → personal_interview → offer | rejected | ghosted`

Interviews are pushed to Google Calendar automatically. Every stage change is
recorded as an event, feeding live stats — response rate, apps this week,
average days to first response, in-flight count — onto an **extra-large macOS
desktop widget** and a native app.

## How it's built

- **Ingestion pipeline** (Python, launchd every 3h) reads labelled Gmail,
  sends each email to **Claude (Opus) with a structured-output schema**, and
  upserts results into SQLite — with logic that never regresses a stage and
  auto-derives "ghosted" after 21 quiet days.
- **MCP server** lets me add and move applications by hand straight from
  Claude Desktop.
- **Google Calendar integration** creates interview events.
- **macOS app + widget** render the pipeline, post notifications on stage
  changes, and allow moving applications between stages.

## Why it matters

It's a real, multi-component system: an event-sourced data model, an LLM in
the classification loop, OAuth integrations, a background scheduler, and a
native client. It's the project I reach for when someone asks what I can
actually ship end-to-end.
